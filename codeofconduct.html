TAMU ASTRO GROUP

Code of Conduct

The Astronomy group at TAMU is committed to creating and maintaining a professional and respectful environment. This code of conduct applies to all people affiliated with the Astronomy group.

Guidelines

  • Respect professional, physical, and personal boundaries.
  • Be respectful of the opinions, views, and rights of others.
  • Respect the pronouns that individuals use to identify themselves.
  • Criticisms should be constructive and professional, and should aim for a positive outcome.
  • Be aware of power differentials between people at different academic stages (undergraduate, graduate, postdoc, staff, faculty). Act to protect and empower academically younger individuals.
  • Support those who report violations of departmental or university policy.
  • Speak up if you witness unacceptable behavior and/or report it afterward.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

  • Harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or retaliation in any form.
  • Physical or verbal abuse of anyone, such as disparaging comments related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, physical and mental disability, physical appearance or body size, age, socio-economic status, veteran status, race, color, religion, national origin, or marital/domestic partnership status.
  • Making microaggressions, stereotypes, and unwelcome jokes.
  • Unwelcome physical contact.
  • All behavior considered inappropriate or prohibited under Title IX.

Reporting and resolving incidents

If you encounter unacceptable behavior, please document and report it.

  • You can report incidents to people in the department, including: your academic supervisor, Dr. Grigory Rogachev (department chair, rogachev@tamu.edu), Sherree Kessler (academic advisor, skessler@tamu.edu), George Welch (Climate & Diversity committee chair, grw@physics.grwelch.com).
    • The people listed here can keep your report confidential at your request, pending institutional or legal reporting requirements. However, anonymity may limit remediation options.
  • You can report incidents online to the Climate & Diversity committee’s Anonymous Comments & Suggestions form. However, the anonymity of the form may limit remediation options.
  • You can report incidents to TAMU’s Title IX office (civilrights@tamu.edu), including online with TAMU’s Title IX reporting website, or to Jennifer Smith, JD (TAMU Title IX officer, jennifer.smith@tamu.edu).
  • You can report incidents involving hate/bias at TAMU's online Stop Hate webpage.
  • An ombuds officer can also confidentially help with conflict resolution.

For more information about how to report incidents to Title IX, see the TAMU Title IX FAQ page.
