Life in Bryan/College Station

1. Housing? Housing!

The Mitchell Building for Fundamental Astronomy and Physics (where all of the astronomy faculty and grad students have offices) is at the very northern edge of campus. If you’re arriving in B/CS without a car, it’s a good idea to select an apartment in the Northgate area, which is just a ten minute walk from our building. The cheapest you can find for a one bedroom apartment is around 600 dollars (such as this apartment complex, https://www.centuryoakbcs.com/). A handful of the astronomy graduate students live in The Northgate Lofts for 725 dollars a month as well.

There are also a number of large scale apartment complexes just across the street from our building, such as Cherry Street Apartments and The Tradition, some of which include amenities such as a gym and pool. However, in general graduate students do not choose to live in these sorts of places because they are predominantly occupied by undergraduates and more expensive than the smaller apartments a few blocks north (~1000 dollars for a one bedroom).

If you’re arriving to B/CS with a vehicle, quite a few of the graduate students live in apartments in the Wolf Penn Creek area. Typical rent costs around (Jon said 900ish for his 2 bedroom).

2. Where do people usually go to do their grocery shopping?

For those who are close to campus without a car, the nearest store is a CVS across the street from the Mitchell building. You can purchase toiletries, snacks, medicine, frozen foods, etc. For fresh food, there is the Farm Patch Produce Market on College Avenue, which is just a 10 minute bike ride from Northgate. There are also two H-E-B grocery stores (one on Villa Maria Rd, another on Texas Ave) that are also a 10 minute bike away.

For those with a car, there are 5 H-E-Bs (all hail our grocer overlord), 3 Wal-Marts, 2 Targets, and 2 Krogers. We suggest doing your grocery shopping on game days during the game.
